Actually for quite a large amount of people now in China, it is even easier to understand women’s over-behaviors towards men in such a men-dominated culture, than show empathy to people who directly resist other forms of power in the society. This looks contradictory but at the same time realistic in some sense. It may be truly hard to understand for people who live out of this country and apart from eastern socialism, though I  firmly believe this reveals some fundamental features of Communist China. People only care about what concerns to themselves, and pay little attention to other aspects of the lack of human rights which seem distant or irrelevant to them. For example, people who appeal to promote rights and interests for delivery men may not call him/herself a feminist, and at the same time feminists in Communist China calling for lifting status of career women even pay no attention to overseas students who have trouble in coming back home during Covid-19.

But remember, there is one thing which is inevitable for every single person in China, whatever his/her biological sex is, that he/she must be regarded as female, and be gazed by the sight of the huge state machine, which can be personified as male. If one justify the reasonability of that kind of gaze, then what is done under the name of equality will all be swallowed conversely in some sense.
