Actually for quite a large segment of people now in China, it is even easier to understand women’s over-behaviors towards men in such a men-dominated cultural community, than show empathy to people who directly resist such a power-dominated society. This looks contradictory but at the same time realistic in some sense. It may be truly hard to understand for people who live out of this country and apart from eastern socialism, though I  firmly believe this reveals some fundamental features of Communist China. People only care about what concerns to themselves, and pay not attention to other aspects of the lack of human rights which seem irrelevant to them. For example, people who appeal to promote rights and interests for delivery men may not call him/herself a feminist, and feminists calling for lifting status of career women even pay no attention to overseas students who have trouble in coming back home.
But remember, there is one thing which is inevitable for every single normal one in China, whatever his/her biological sex is, that he/she must be regarded as female, and be gazed by the sight of the huge state machine. If one justify the reasonability of that kind of gaze, then what is done under the name of equality will all be swallowed conversely in some sense.
